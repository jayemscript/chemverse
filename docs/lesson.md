# Chemistry Lessons Curriculum

This document outlines a comprehensive chemistry curriculum suitable for high school to early undergraduate levels. It can be used for creating lessons, quizzes, or structured learning paths.

---

## 1. Introduction / Basics

- What is Chemistry?
- Matter, Elements, Compounds, Mixtures
- States of Matter and Their Properties
- Measurement & Units in Chemistry
- Scientific Method in Chemistry

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Central Science by Brown et al.
- General Chemistry I & II by Zumdahl

---

## 2. Atomic Structure

- History of Atomic Theory (Dalton, Thomson, Rutherford, Bohr)
- Subatomic Particles: Protons, Neutrons, Electrons
- Atomic Number, Mass Number, Isotopes
- Electron Configuration
- Quantum Numbers & Orbitals
- Periodic Trends (Atomic Radius, Ionization Energy, Electronegativity)

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Central Science
- Inorganic Chemistry by Shriver & Atkins

---

## 3. Periodic Table

- Organization of the Periodic Table
- Groups & Periods
- Metals, Non-Metals, Metalloids
- Alkali Metals, Alkaline Earth Metals, Transition Metals, Halogens, Noble Gases
- Periodic Trends (reactivity, electronegativity)

**Reference Books:**

- General Chemistry by Ebbing & Gammon

---

## 4. Chemical Bonding

- Ionic Bonds
- Covalent Bonds
- Polar vs Non-Polar Molecules
- Metallic Bonds
- Intermolecular Forces (Hydrogen Bonds, Dipole-Dipole, London Dispersion)
- Molecular Shapes (VSEPR Theory)

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Molecular Nature of Matter by Silberberg
- Chemistry: The Central Science

---

## 5. Stoichiometry & Chemical Reactions

- Balancing Chemical Equations
- Mole Concept & Molar Mass
- Empirical & Molecular Formulas
- Types of Reactions (Synthesis, Decomposition, Single/Double Replacement, Combustion)
- Limiting Reactant & Percent Yield

**Reference Books:**

- General Chemistry by Ebbing & Gammon

---

## 6. Acids, Bases, and pH

- Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, Lewis Concepts
- Strong vs Weak Acids/Bases
- pH & pOH Calculations
- Buffers
- Titration & Indicators

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Central Science
- Principles of Chemistry by Atkins & Jones

---

## 7. Thermodynamics

- Energy, Heat, Work
- Enthalpy (ΔH), Entropy (ΔS), Gibbs Free Energy (ΔG)
- Exothermic vs Endothermic Reactions
- Spontaneity & Equilibrium
- Hess’s Law

**Reference Books:**

- Physical Chemistry by Peter Atkins

---

## 8. Kinetics & Chemical Equilibrium

- Rate of Reaction
- Factors Affecting Reaction Rate (Concentration, Temperature, Catalyst)
- Rate Laws & Reaction Order
- Dynamic Equilibrium
- Le Chatelier’s Principle

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Central Science
- Physical Chemistry by Atkins

---

## 9. Redox Reactions & Electrochemistry

- Oxidation & Reduction
- Identifying Oxidizing and Reducing Agents
- Electrochemical Cells
- Standard Electrode Potentials

**Reference Books:**

- Electrochemistry by Bard & Faulkner
- General Chemistry by Ebbing & Gammon

---

## 10. Organic Chemistry

- Introduction to Organic Molecules
- Functional Groups (Alcohols, Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids, Amines, Esters)
- Nomenclature (IUPAC)
- Alkane, Alkene, Alkyne Chemistry
- Isomerism (Structural & Stereoisomerism)
- Common Reactions (Substitution, Addition, Elimination, Oxidation)

**Reference Books:**

- Organic Chemistry by Paula Y. Bruice
- Organic Chemistry by Morrison & Boyd

---

## 11. Solutions & Colligative Properties

- Solubility & Saturation
- Concentration Units (Molarity, Molality, Normality)
- Raoult’s Law
- Freezing Point Depression, Boiling Point Elevation

**Reference Books:**

- General Chemistry by Ebbing & Gammon

---

## 12. Nuclear Chemistry

- Radioactivity (α, β, γ decay)
- Half-life & Decay Calculations
- Nuclear Reactions & Fission/Fusion

**Reference Books:**

- Chemistry: The Central Science
- Inorganic Chemistry by Shriver & Atkins

---

## 13. Analytical Chemistry & Instrumental Methods

- Gravimetric & Volumetric Analysis
- Spectroscopy (UV-Vis, IR, NMR)
- Chromatography (TLC, GC, HPLC)

**Reference Books:**

- Quantitative Chemical Analysis by Daniel Harris

---

_End of Curriculum_
